DailyOffice · UI Kit · v1.0

A dense, dark workspace for back-of-house operators.

DailyOffice is a unified back-office for consumer brands — inventory, PackStation, challans, vendors, petty-cash — all on one glass-dark surface. This kit is the distilled visual language behind that product: six accent colours, three typefaces, twelve hand-drawn icons, and a density-first 12 px base grid.

00 — PRINCIPLES

Four rules we return to.

Design decisions in DailyOffice are not a matter of taste; they’re a matter of fit. The app is used for eight hours a day on laptops in back-rooms and warehouses. Every token in this kit earns its keep by serving one of the four ideas below.

Authored byarya.designs
01

Density first

12 px is the base — labels go as low as 8 px with heavy tracking. Show more on one screen so operators scroll less. Whitespace is a tool, not a default.

02

Dark, not decorative

A single near-black surface (#060810) with four subtly-lifted glass panels. Indigo is the only chromatic accent the UI chases; everything else earns its colour by encoding state.

03

State through colour

Five semantic hues map 1:1 to domain states (complete, damaged, unsorted, dry-clean, info). Never invent a sixth to decorate — pick the closest state or use tertiary grey.

04

Numbers are signage

Stat numbers wear Sora 700, tabular-nums, 20 px. They carry the visual weight of a section header because operators read KPIs before prose.

01 — BRAND

The gradient dot and the word after it.

Two marks, same DNA: a 135° indigo→periwinkle gradient with a soft glow. The standalone dot carries the app on dense headers; the wordmark takes over in ambient spaces — auth, empty-states, onboarding.

03 — TYPE

Sora for signage, Inter for sentences, Mono for machines.

Three families with a hard split of responsibilities. Sora appears only at 700/800 for display and numeric signage; Inter covers everything else at 400–600; JetBrains Mono is used exclusively for IDs, serials, SKUs, and currency alignment in tables.

04 — SHAPE & SPACE

Small radii. Smaller gaps. Quiet shadows.

Radii top out at 14 px (only the login card hits 18 px). Gaps live in the 4–20 px range. The only loud shadow is the primary-button indigo glow — modals and toasts use near-black drop shadows so the accent stays the only chromatic light source.

06 — USAGE RULES

Short list. Follow it.

Most bugs in a design system come from people re-inventing a decision that had already been made. These six lines are the invariants. Everything else is negotiable.

Applies toall new screens
01
Gradient is brand only

Reserve --do-ac-grad for the wordmark, the primary CTA, the active-nav pip, and the login hero. Never fill illustrations, backgrounds, or charts with it.

02
State colour or grey — nothing new

If the data isn’t Complete, Damaged, Unsorted, Dry-clean, Info or Cash-in-hand, it takes --do-tx-3. Don’t pick a sixth hue to “feel different”.

03
Mono is for machines

--do-mono is IDs, SKUs, serials, currency columns. Labels and running copy stay Inter; display still goes to Sora.

04
Radii cap at 14 px

Inputs/buttons at 6 px. Cards at 8–10 px. Modals at 14 px. Only the unauthenticated login card earns 18 px.

05
Tag recipe is fixed

10% bg · 25% border · light-tint fg · 9/600 caps. Don’t raise opacity to “make it pop” — raise copy clarity instead.

06
12 px base is non-negotiable

The whole grid is wound to 12 px HTML. Don’t set font-size: 14px on sections — pick a larger step from the scale (13/15/20).
